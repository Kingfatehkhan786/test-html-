The Legends Ranking Top 10 Legendary batters, powered by Wisden

For cricketing fans, there is no end to the satisfaction they earn watching the replay of their favorite match moments. It won't be wrong to say they can spend hours on a stretch watching previous match highlights with the same gusto and enthusiasm as a live match. And when these highlights concern their favorite retired cricket players, it becomes even more refreshing. However, now game enthusiasts don't only have to suffice their love for cricket with memorable shots, big hits, and marvelous strikes. They have another ranking to follow and more matches to watch that too of their favorite retired cricketing legends, with Legends Ranking.

This new ranking system is wholly devoted to the retired players of cricket and tracks their performance in comparison with the overall team and opponent to decipher their world ranking. Although this ranking is similar to the ICC Cricket Ranking, the only difference is its focus on retired cricket players than active ones. For its excellent format, the Legends Ranking has earned quite a reputation and stardom for itself, especially with the display of fantastic gameplay by some of the most celebrated cricketing legendaries in the recently concluded Legends League Cricket T20 (LLC20). The Legends Ranking system empowers retired cricket players to stay at the top of the charts even after retirement from all formats of international cricket. And this aspiration is what blesses cricket fans with yet more fantastic game moments and memories of their favorite cricket players.

The Legends Ranking system and how it functions

The Legends Ranking powered by Wisden is a table where international cricket legends' performances are ranked using a points-based system which is worked out by doing a series of calculations leading to a more concrete moving average. Players are rated on a scale depending on how many leagues they play, the overall performance of their team, and that of the opponent. If a player's performance is better than his past record, the points increase, and the rank improves; if their performance declines, their points and rank will go down.

The score of each player's performance within a match is calculated via an algorithm that involves a series of calculations based on different match circumstances. As this calculation is entirely automated and does not involve any human indulgence, no subjective assessment is concluded. All in all, the Legends Ranking depends on the player's performance since his retirement in any national and international league.

The top 10 legendary batters as per Legends Ranking, powered by Wisden

Rank #1: Kevin Pietersen

For many cricket fans in England, no name sparks excitement like Kevin Pietersen. His flamboyant master play has been regarded as one of the finest performances for a decade. His international career ended in 2014, and Pietersen has been a part of several prominent leagues like IPL, Melbourne Stars, Caribbean Premier League, Ultimate Kricket Challenge, and a Bicentenary Celebration match at the Lord's. Even post his retirement, Kevin Pietersen has kept his captivating game style alive.

Rank #2: Tillakaratne Dilshan

TM Dilshan is known for his relentless aggression, defensive play, intense play, and natural timing. From the time he made his debut, Dilshan has been known as a destructive opener and a brilliant counter-attacker. Apart from international cricket, Dilshan also made headlines while playing leagues such as IPL, the Road Safety World Series, etc.

Rank #3: Upul Tharanga

Upul Tharanga has had an illustrious player in the squad, a former Sri Lankan cricketer and a former limited-overs captain. Spotted young, Tharanga has delivered accomplished performances in ODI and Test Cricket. His stellar experience makes him a vital cog in the Sri Lankan team.

Rank #4: Virender Sehwag

With an extraordinary career and a genius for boundary-hitting, Virender Sehwag is known for minimal footwork and maximum-intent gameplay. He is known to pile test runs faster than anyone in the history of cricket. His ability to amass massive scores at breathtaking speed has made him hold Indian records for the highest number of test double-hundreds. He can now be seen sharing his views as a commentator candidly.

Rank #5: Yusuf Pathan

With an incredible strike rate, Yusuf Pathan is a talented Indian limited-overs middle overs. He is one of the hardest hitters in domestic and international cricket. He is capable of giving a massive push to the innings during slog overs and has earned himself a reputation in T20 cricket, especially IPL.

Rank #6: Naman Ojha

Naman Ojha has been a phenomenal name in the domestic circuit. Post his impressive stint in the Challenger Trophy, Ojha has been a part of the 2016 IPL-winning Sunrisers Hyderabad team.

Rank #7: Irfan Pathan

Pathan is one of the most talented swings and seam bowlers since the Kapil Dev era. With a couple of years in international cricket, he is regarded as one of the most talented all-rounders. He remains a sought-after name at the IPL and has had stints with Kings XI Punjab, Delhi Daredevils, Sunrisers Hyderabad, and Chennai Super Kings.

Rank #8: Sachin Tendulkar

Regarded as one of the most incredible batters in the history of cricket, Sachin Tendulkar is an all-time highest run-scorer in both ODI and Test formats. After retirement, he captained the Bicentenary celebration match at Lord's and was made the icon player and caption for Mumbai Indians in IPL.

Rank #9: Andrew Puttick

A left-handed opening batsman, Andrew Puttick has consistently performed in domestic cricket. He immensely contributed to the Cobras' MTN Domestic Championship and became the first-ever batsman to score a century in the Champions League T20.

Rank #10: Dwayne Smith

Dwayne Smith is known for his high backlift, Calypso-style batting, strong shots over mid-wicket, and powerful drives. As an all-rounder, he has graced several T20 tournaments, including the Indian Premier League, the Big Bash, Bangladesh Premier League, and Pakistan Super League.
