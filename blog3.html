The Legends Ranking Top 10 Legendary batters, powered by Wisden

It is rightly said cricket is not a game; it is an emotion. And this emotion means the world to cricket fans. Be it live extravaganza or adrenaline-pumping match highlights; fans just can never get enough of the thrill and excitement of the game. While some might applaud the batters, others are impressed by the bowling attack. Regardless of who your favorite player is, when any cricketer retires, it feels like the end of the era. And this is why, whenever your favorite player makes a comeback in domestic or league matches, it is a moment of utmost joy and enthralment for their fans. And it is precisely this emotion that the Legends Ranking zeroes in.

Since the conclusion of the Legends League Cricket, it has become rather noticeable that even post-retirement, cricket players have a lot of games left within them. Moreover, the fans love to watch their favorite players on screens, hitting those massive numbers, masquerading with their balls to take those wickets. And that is what commenced the stardom around Legends Ranking.

What is Legends Ranking?

The Legends Ranking system is designed to accurately gauge the ranking of the world's retired players and teams. Like the ICC Ranking system focussed on rating the active players of the cricketing arenas, these rankings concentrate on the retired international players who still have a lot of sport left in them and are seen playing their master strokes in various leagues and tournaments. Legends Ranking currently works across the T20 format and is released for retired cricketing legends.

What is the Legends Ranking system?

If we were to ask you you're most disheartening moment, it would surely be when you had to listen to that speech or read a post of your favorite cricket player where they announced their retirement from different cricket formats. While you all love to celebrate and keep the gameplay of the legendary cricket players alive, several of them commence playing the second stint of their career with domestic and league cricket. And this is precisely what Legends Ranking system is all about.

In all its forms, Legends Ranking inspires retired cricket legends to keep going and look forward to something else than international cricket. And for retired celebrated players who feel domestic and league cricket is not worth it, it showcases the real power of this cricket format to them, how into their fans are, and how much they adore watching their favorite players play in the stadiums again. Banking on the adage, A legend always remains a legend," this ranking system allocates positions on the charts to all the retired cricket players who still care about their fans, want to display their game, and never miss a moment to get and stay on top.

Depending on the camaraderie of the retired players and the legends who still make it up in the stadiums, the Legends Ranking will note their performance in the game and the league compared with the opponent, and the overall score will be raised, and rank them accordingly. The best part - Legends Ranking provides cricket fans with the opportunity to witness the massive skills of their favorite players and see them rise through the charts even if they have retired from international cricket.

Rank #1: Nuwan Kulasekara

A former Sri Lankan cricketer who played all formats of the game and is considered one of the best swing blowers in the world, Nuwan Kulasekara has played instrumental feats for his home team in the 2014 ICC World Twenty20. He was a part of the team that made it to the finals of the 2007 and 2011 Cricket World Cup and the 2009 and 2012 ICC World Twenty20 Cup. He was appointed bowling coach for a league team in the 2020 Lanka Premier League.

Rank #2: Munaf Patel

A former Indian cricketer known for playing for the West Zone in Duleep Trophy and for Mumbai and Maharashtra in the domestic arena, Munaf Patel has earned significant accolades for his bowling attacks in the domestic circuit. He has been a regular at IPL while playing stints for Mumbai Indians, Rajasthan Royals, and Gujarat Lions.

Rank #3: Muthiah Muralidaran

This Sri Lankan has donned several hats for his home team as a cricket coach and a professional player. For his recognition as one of the most successful and celebrated bowlers in cricket history, Muthiah Muralidaran was also indicted as a member of the ICC Cricket Hall of Fame.

Rank #4: Morne Morkel

Morne Morkel is a South African former cricketer known for his domestic circuit career across several leagues, including the Legends League Cricket and Indian Premier League. Morkel was awarded the Legend of the Tournament award for his fantastic gameplay in the former.

Rank #5: Sulieman Benn

This fast bowler from Barbados is known for his slow left-arm orthodox bowling attack. He was a part of the West Indies squad in their 2015 World Cup stint. Since his debut, he had to fight for his place in the West Indies team as it already had enough fast bowlers.

Rank #6: Ryan Sidebottom

Decorated with over 1000 career wickets, Sidebottom is a former England international cricketer known for playing domestic games for Yorkshire and Nottinghamshire. He has won 5 county championships, along with the 2010 ICC World Twenty20 with England.

Rank #7: Irfan Pathan

The younger Pathan is a former Indian cricketer turned commentator and cricket analyst. He is a celebrated all-rounder known for his medium-fast bowling style. His domestic career is as impressive as his international stint with the Indian Premier League and league cups for Baroda and Middlesex.

Rank #8: Manpreet Gony

This Indian cricketer made a mark during the 2008 IPL matches and was one of the leading wicket-takers while performing great with his bat. He was even drafted for the inaugural edition of the Lanka Premier League. He has also been a part of the 2019 Global T20 Canada tournament.

Rank #9: Tillakaratne Dilshan

TM Dilshan is known for his relentless aggression, defensive play, intense play, and natural timing. From the time he made his debut, Dilshan has been known as a destructive opener and a brilliant counter-attacker. Apart from international cricket, Dilshan also made headlines while playing leagues such as IPL, the Road Safety World Series, etc.

Rank #10: Monty Panesar

A left-arm spinner is known for his immense domestic experience with the Sussex County Cricket Club, the Ashes, and his international stint.
